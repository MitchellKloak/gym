Your Personal BMR:

Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the amount of energy your body needs to function while at rest, to maintain basic physiological functions like breathing, circulating blood, and regulating body temperature. It's basically the number of calories your body burns at rest just to keep you alive.
Your Personal IBW:

IBW stands for Ideal Body Weight. It's an estimate of the weight that is considered healthy for an individual based on factors like height, gender, and body frame size.
Your Personal TDEE:

TDEE stands for Total Daily Energy Expenditure. It's the total number of calories your body burns in a day, taking into account your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) plus any additional calories burned through physical activity and digestion. Essentially, it's the sum of all the energy your body uses in a day.
Your Personal BMI:

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It's a measure used to assess a person's body weight in relation to their height. It's calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters. It's a commonly used screening tool to determine whether someone is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
